Background

In this mini-project, you will explore FiveThirtyEight’s Halloween candy set. FiveThirtyEight, sometimes rendered as just 538, is an American website that focuses mostly on opinion poll analysis, politics, econimics, and sports blogging. We will ggplot some basic stats, correlation analysis and PCA to make sense of the landscape of US candy.

Q16. This is quite ugly, use the reorder() function to get the bars sorted by winpercent?

ggplot(candy) +
  aes(winpercent, rownames(candy)) +
  geom_col() +
  ylab("")
my_cols=rep("black", nrow(candy))
my_cols[as.logical(candy$chocolate)] = "chocolate"
my_cols[as.logical(candy$bar)] = "brown"
my_cols[as.logical(candy$fruity)] = "pink"
ggplot(candy) +
  aes(winpercent, reorder(rownames(candy), winpercent)) +
  geom_col(fill=my_cols)
